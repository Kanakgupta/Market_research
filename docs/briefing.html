Matter and OpenADR Collaboration for Grid-Connected Smart Homes

The Connectivity Standards Alliance (CSA) and the OpenADR Alliance have announced a liaison agreement to collaborate on grid-connected energy management. This partnership aims to simplify smart home energy management by enabling devices to communicate with the electricity grid. This integration is crucial for demand response programs and optimizing energy consumption in smart homes.

Watch list

Matter 1.5 Specification

Upcoming features in Matter 1.5 (cameras, energy, water valves, robots) will define new device categories and drive demand for compatible chipsets. Infineon's AIROC portfolio needs to align with these evolving standards.

Wi-Fi 7 Adoption in IoT

While Wi-Fi 7 is gaining traction in mobile and PC, its adoption in the IoT space is still nascent. Competitors like Synaptics and MediaTek are pushing Wi-Fi 7 solutions, requiring Infineon to strategize its Wi-Fi 7 roadmap for IoT.

Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast Deployments

The increasing focus on LE Audio and Auracast by competitors like Ceva and Qualcomm necessitates a strong offering from Infineon to capture market share in premium audio and public broadcasting applications.

Energy Harvesting BLE Solutions

Companies like Atmosic are innovating in battery-less IoT devices. Monitoring this trend is important for identifying niche opportunities and potential future requirements for ultra-low-power solutions.

RISC-V adoption in IoT

Vendors like Bouffalo Lab are leveraging RISC-V for their IoT chips. Understanding the ecosystem growth and performance of RISC-V based solutions is crucial for competitive analysis.
